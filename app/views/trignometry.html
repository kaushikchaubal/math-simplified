Trigonometry means Measurement of Triangles. We shall deal with right angled triangles and the relation between their angles and sides in this unit
In the diagrams below, let ∠ θ be the angle of reference for the trigonometric ratios
Trigonometric Ratios:
(Sine) sin θ = Opposite Side/Hypotenuse = y/z
(Cosine) cos θ = Adjacent Side/Hypotenuse = x/z
(Tangent) tan θ = Opposite Side/Adjacent Side = y/x
(Co-Tangent) cot θ = Adjacent Side/Opposite Side = x/y
(Secant) sec θ = Hypotenuse/Adjacent Side = z/x
(Co-Secant) cosec θ = Hypotenuse/Opposite Side = z/y
Hence from the above definitions we can derive the Reciprocal Relations:
tan = sin/cos
cot = cos/sin
tan = 1/cot
cot = 1/tan
sec = 1/cos
cosec = 1/sin
Ratios of angles 30o and 60o
Sin 30 = s/2s = ½ Cos 30 = h/2s = (√3/2)2s/2s = √3/2
Tan 30 = sin 30/cos 30 = 1/√3 Cot 30 = 1/tan 30 = √3
Sec 30 = 1/cos 30 = 2/√3 Cosec 30 = 1/sin 30 = 2
Sin 60 = h/2s = (√3/2)2s/2s = √3/2 cos 60 = s/2s = ½
Tan 60 = sin 60/cos 60 = (√3/2)/(1/2) = √3 cot 60 = 1/tan 60 = 1/√3
Sec 60 = 1/cos 60 = 2 cosec 60 = 1/sin 60 = 2/√3
Ratios of angle 45o
Sin 45 = x/x√2 = 1/√2
Cos 45 = x/x√2 = 1/√2
Tan 45 = x/x = 1
Cot 45 = x/x = 1
Sec 45 = 1/cos 45 = √2
Cosec 45 = 1/sin 45 = √2
Trigonometric Identities:
sin2θ + cos2θ = 1
sec2θ = 1 + tan2θ
cosec2θ = 1+ cot2θ
cos (90 - θ) = sin θ sin (90 - θ) = cos θ
tan (90 - θ) = cot θ cot (90 - θ) = tan θ
sec (90 - θ) = cosec θ cosec (90 - θ) = sec θ
Using the trigonometric ratios we can prove the Identities:
(Sine) sin θ = Opposite Side/Hypotenuse = y/z
(Cosine) cos θ = Adjacent Side/Hypotenuse = x/z
Also according to Pythagoras Theorem, x2 + y2 = z2
Sin2θ = y2/z2; cos2θ = x2/z2
Sin2θ + cos2θ = (y2 + x2)/z2 = z2/z2
Hence, sin2θ + cos2θ = 1
